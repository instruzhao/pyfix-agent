## Repository-context observations

- All 19 repair-context builds emitted trace schema 1.3 repository metadata.
- The largest selected source context was estimated at 1,021 tokens; no case reached the 12,000-token release budget.
- `multi_file_discount` followed a direct dependency edge and added `src/rules.py` outside the traceback/direct-test seed set.
- The sklearn Iris repair used reverse importer edges to add `tests/test_data.py` and `tests/test_model.py` during its two-step visible-test repair.
- Cache entries remained under the benchmark output directory. Candidate source changes produced new fingerprints before review; three unchanged-fingerprint requests were served from the shared in-memory index.

## Per-case behavior

All cases passed every final gate. Twelve cases converged in one repair iteration. The sklearn Iris case needed one ordinary visible-test retry. `file_extension` used one semantic revision, while `decimal_invoice` used two. Those two v0.5.1 holdout regressions again passed without case-specific task hints.

## Comparison and interpretation

The one-run v0.6.0 and v0.6.1 samples both reached 15/15 final success with zero observed acceptance-classification errors. v0.6.1 increased prompt characters from 128,999 to 141,963 while total model tokens decreased slightly from 121,785 to 119,180; stochastic model behavior and the small sample prevent attributing that token change to repository context.

This result qualifies feature integration and non-regression on the repository's curated suite. Most cases are deliberately small, so they do not stress the 12,000-token budget or establish broad large-repository quality. Larger genuinely multi-module fixtures remain future benchmark work.
