## Per-case result

Every case passed visible tests, semantic acceptance, candidate holdout evaluation, and the final gate. Thirteen cases converged in one repair iteration. `file_extension` used one semantic revision and converged in two iterations. `decimal_invoice` used the configured maximum of two semantic revisions and converged in three iterations. The `demo_project` candidate was accepted with a non-blocking warning; its holdout also passed.

The two cases that consistently failed the v0.5.1 four-repetition holdout qualification now passed without case-specific task hints:

- `file_extension`: static delimiter-composition analysis caused the reviewer to challenge an already-prefixed extension, and the revised implementation normalized the caller-provided fragment.
- `decimal_invoice`: numeric tie-breaking review led to explicit `ROUND_HALF_UP`; the second revision also supplied a `Decimal` identity for empty sums.

## Interpretation

This run qualifies the v0.6.0 semantic-acceptance path on the repository's 15-case curated benchmark: visible-pass candidates were independently reviewed, bounded revisions were exercised, and there were no observed acceptance-classification errors against the external holdouts. It is one real-model sample, not a statistical claim, an academic benchmark, or evidence of production-grade generality. The previous v0.5.1 release used four repetitions; direct percentage comparisons should account for the smaller v0.6.0 sample.
